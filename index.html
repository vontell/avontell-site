About Me

I am a computer scientist completing my senior year at MIT. I have interested in and have focused on quantum computing, specifically in the way that quantum computers are programmed and used by consumers. My projects and interests span from implementing quantum algorithms and programming processes for the control of nitrogen-vacancy centers in diamond to developing Android applications centered around accessibility for mobile users. I am also an avid moviegoer and reader.

Work

Rigetti Quantum Computing

Junior Quantum Engineer (Intern) • June 2017 - August 2017 (Berekely, CA)

As an intern, I implemented quantum algorithms using Rigetti's Quil instruction set, such as Shor's algorithm, oracular algorithms, amplitude amplification, the inverse quantum Fourier transform, and superdense coding. I also completed work on compiler optimization and analytics for quantum circuits and programs, as well as a demo for the launch of Forest 1.0.

MIT RLE Quantum Photonics Laboratory

Undergraduate Researcher • September 2016 - Present (Cambdrige, MA)

Under the advisement of Dirk Englund, this research project required the configuration, development, and testing of a customizable suite of tools geared toward single qubit operations in the form of Nitrogen-vacancy centers in diamond. Using ARTIQ to write FPGA code in Python, I made a pulse sequence abstraction which enables qubit initialization and arbitrary pulse sequence generation. The overall goal of the project is to support the development of a quantum repeater.

Papers and Publications

Utility programs for nitrogen-vacancy center identification and initialization analysis

Aaron Vontell, Michael Walsh, Eric Wadkins, Dirk Englund
Internal use only, not for publication

Here we present an automated tool utilizing TensorFlow to train and classify spectra scans to determine if the scan is characteristic of a nitrogen-vacancy center (NV). We also present a process using ARTIQ to determine characteristics of our NV initialization, determined by photon counts.

Pulse sequence generation and input echoing using ARTIQ and the observer pattern

Aaron Vontell, Michael Walsh, Dirk Englund
Internal use only, not for publication

Here we present a suite of tools for real time quantum information processingusing ARTIQ, for the purpose of generating pulse sequences and echoing input signals in thecontext of Nitrogen vacancy centers in diamond.
